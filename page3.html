Dangers of Riding

Riding motorcycles involves many dangers. Traffic, road debris, and even your own riding ability can all cause you to crash and have a bad time.

Traffic dangers

Other cars are usually the number one most dangerous thing to a motorcyclist on the road. They can suddenly change lanes and hit you off your bike within a fraction of a second. It's advised to slow and use extreme caution around traffic or any single vehicle on the road.

Road debris

Rocks, potholes, sand, gravel, leaves, trees, and even deer are common forms of road debris encounted by motorcyclists. All of these things can easily lead to a crash, especially at night when it's much harder to examine the road ahead, and especially around turns.

Self-evaluated riding ability

This one can get you into a lot of trouble. Confidence of how well you're able to control the bike in certain situations can be severly over-estimated or misjudged. Crashes often happen because the rider goes as fast as possible, not leaving any room for error or changing external factors, which inevitably leads to a loss of control, and ususally a crash.
